National ACT scores vs. national depression

Studies prove there is a direct correlation with intelligence and mental health problems. Students with higher test scores are more likely to develop a mental illness. The Healthy Minds Study releases an annual report of statistics of mental health problems on college campuses. This graph compares national ACT scores and diagnosed depression among college students.

Beginning in 2012, national ACT scores raised in correlation with the increase cases of diagnosed depression. Within three years, cases of major depression increased by 3 percent. However, both statistics slightly dropped in 2016.
