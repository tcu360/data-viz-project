Economic Impacts of Trinity River Vision Development

The Trinity River Vision aims to connect neighborhoods and prevent flooding with the creation of a modern, waterfront community, according to the Trinity River Vision Authority. The Trinity River Vision will be located between Downtown Fort Worth, the Cultural District and the Stockyards. This project will be similar to the San Antonio River Walk. In October 2003, the Trinity River Vision plan was approved by the city of Fort Worth and is now in the cityâ€™s comprehensive plan. The project is expected to be complete in 2045.

The river project will reconnect downtown Fort Worth with the Trinity River in hopes of encouraging activity on the water. People will be able to kayak, canoe, and use rowboats on the water. In addition, small ferries will run on the main water bodies to carry people to different points in the city. To avoid flooding, the city plans to create a bypass channel that will direct flood water away from residential sites with three food control gates.
