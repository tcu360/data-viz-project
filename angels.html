Gender Makeup of Colleges

College campuses have seen a decades-long shift in the gender gap of their student bodies, which recently settled at a 60/40 split in favor of women. Even 15 years ago, the absence of men at the university level was being noticed in classes, on campus and in dating culture. Perhaps this shift is due to changing gender roles in society: men have become more comfortable staying home while more women seek college degrees. Maybe men have weighed the affordability of a college education and don’t think it’s worth it. Some universities embrace this growing disparity, some see no problem with it. On TCU’s campus the male-to-female ratio is 59 percent female to 41 percent male, according to the admissions office. The exploration of the gender ratio at TCU goes beyond the admissions level. Some colleges enroll much larger percentages of one gender over the other. The first the come to mind may be the College of Nursing or the College of Education. Five tcu360.com reporters explored the gender ratio of each college and looked deeper to find which majors or departments were home to mostly females or males.
